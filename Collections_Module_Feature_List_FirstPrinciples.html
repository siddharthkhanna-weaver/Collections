Affordable Housing Finance · Collections

Collections Module — Feature List

Derived from first principles. Every feature must serve one of seven atomic jobs. Anything that doesn't is bloat.

Method

A collections module exists for one reason: close the gap between what is owed and what is collected — legally, economically, at scale.

From that single purpose, seven atomic jobs fall out. Two cross-cutting layers — guardrails (compliance, consent, security) and plumbing (integrations, master data, configuration) — wrap all seven.

Features below are derived from the jobs they serve. If a feature can't be tied back to a job, it's bloat — push back on it during vendor evaluation.
// The Seven Atomic Jobs

What a Collections Module Must Do

Each job answers one fundamental question. Every feature in the rest of this document maps back to one of these seven.

JOB · 01
Sense
What is the current state of every account, borrower, contact, and asset?
JOB · 02
Decide
For each case, at this moment, what is the right next action?
JOB · 03
Reach
Can we contact this borrower — through which channel, when?
JOB · 04
Act
Execute the chosen treatment — nudge, call, visit, notice, suit, auction.
JOB · 05
Record
What happened, who did it, with what evidence?
JOB · 06
Settle
Collect the money, reconcile it, close the case correctly.
JOB · 07
Improve
Measure outcomes, attribute causes, tune the strategy.
JOB 01

Sense — Know the State

A system cannot collect what it cannot see. The minimum sensing surface:

1.1 Account & loan state

  • Loan master — product, sanction, schedule, tenor, ROI, EMI, collateral linkage Loan LN10234: ₹18L sanctioned, 240-month tenor, 11.5% ROI, EMI ₹19,180, secured by Plot 47/B Bhiwadi.
  • Live ledger — principal, interest accrued, penal, charges, suspense, paid/due/skipped EMIs On 26-Apr Mr. Sharma's account shows principal ₹16.4L, interest accrued ₹4,200, penal ₹680, 1 EMI overdue, ₹0 in suspense.
  • DPD calculator (running, not batch) with bucket assignment EMI due 5-Apr; today 26-Apr → DPD = 21 → bucket 1-30. Recomputed every hour as payments post — no overnight wait.
  • Roll/flow tracking — yesterday's bucket → today's bucket per account Yesterday 142 accounts in 0-DPD; 11 of them rolled to 1-30 today because their NACH presentation bounced overnight.
  • Restructure / re-age / moratorium flags — both original and revised schedules retained Borrower restructured Aug-2024 due to job loss: original EMI ₹22,000, revised ₹17,000 for 18mo. Both schedules retained for RBI audit.

1.2 Borrower & relationship state

  • Borrower 360 — applicant, co-applicant, guarantor, references, employer, all linked accounts One screen shows Mr. Sharma (applicant) + Mrs. Sharma (co-applicant) + brother (guarantor), plus his ₹2L personal loan from same lender — cross-default visible.
  • Identity graph — PAN, Aadhaar masked, mobile, email, address, with verification status + source-of-truth per attribute PAN ABCDE1234F verified via NSDL; mobile 9XXX-XX12 verified via OTP 14-Mar; address sourced from registered sale deed.
  • Behavioral history — past delinquencies, cures, PTPs kept/broken, settlements, complaints, hardship flags Bounced Jan-2024 cured Day-9; broke 1 PTP Feb-2025; raised an RM-conduct complaint Aug-2025 → auto-flag "handle gently, no aggressive scripts".
  • KYC / contact freshness — age of last refresh, auto-trigger periodic refresh Last KYC refresh 18 months ago → RBI re-KYC due → branch task auto-created for next visit.

1.3 Collateral state (mortgage-secured)

  • Property record — address, geo-coordinates, type, current valuation, last valuation date, valuer ID Plot 47/B Sector-3 Bhiwadi 301019, lat 28.20 lng 76.86, independent house, ₹24L (Knight Frank, Apr-2026).
  • Lien status, insurance policy, premium-paid status, claim history Equitable mortgage registered 12-Jan-2022; HDFC Ergo property cover policy HE-882 expires 14-Aug-2026, premium paid; no claims to date.
  • Title document custody — location + barcode/RFID Original sale deed in Bhiwadi-Branch vault, locker B-104, RFID tag 0x4F2A; last accessed 10-Mar-2026 by Branch Manager (logged).

1.4 Contactability state

  • Per-channel reachability — RNR/switched-off rate, bounce rate, PIN-validity, last-successful-contact timestamp Mobile-1: RNR 8/10 last attempts; Mobile-2: 60% connect; email: bounced 22-Apr; address PIN valid → strategy engine drops Mobile-1 + email for now.
  • Consent registry per channel — DND, WhatsApp opt-in, voice-bot consent, recording consent WhatsApp opt-in YES (12-Mar-2024); voice DND after 6pm; recording-consent YES captured in 14-Apr call audio at 00:08.
  • Working-hours profile — when this borrower actually picks up Best connect Tue-Thu 11am-1pm based on last 30 days; avoid Sat (visits temple). Dialer auto-respects.

1.5 Field & operational state

  • Geo-mesh of cases — pincode / cluster / branch view of the portfolio Bhiwadi cluster: 432 cases — 38 in 1-30, 12 in 31-60, 4 in 61-90, 2 in 90+ — assigned to FOS team of 6.
  • Agent / agency capacity, in-shift status, route load Agent Ravi K — 8 active visits today, capacity 12, in-shift since 9:42am, currently at lat 28.21/76.85.
  • Notice & legal-stage state machine — where each case sits in the legal pipeline LN10234 currently at SARFAESI 13(2) issued 10-Apr; 60-day reply window expires 9-Jun; auto-task to lawyer for 13(4) draft on 10-Jun.

1.6 Event stream

  • Append-only event log of every state transition — single source of truth; reports are projections, not separate tables Every payment, bucket roll, contact attempt, PTP, notice issued lands as an immutable event. Any moment in account history can be replayed; new reports are queries, not new pipelines.
Why first-principles: without 1.1–1.6, jobs 2–7 are guesswork. Most "missing feature" complaints in collection systems trace back to a hole in the sensing surface.
JOB 02

Decide — Choose the Right Action

For this case, now, what is the cheapest action with the highest expected recovery?

2.1 Segmentation

  • Rule-based (DPD × ticket × product × geography × employment-type) DPD 31-60 + ticket >₹15L + self-employed + Tier-3 city → segment HARD-FIELD-PRIO with daily field touch.
  • Score-based (propensity-to-pay, propensity-to-cure, propensity-to-roll) Cure-score 0.78 → light-touch digital is enough; cure-score 0.22 + LGD₹14L → start legal prep in parallel with field.
  • Behavioral (chronic late-payer, first-time defaulter, willful, hardship, contactable-but-unwilling, uncontactable) "Chronic but cures" — 5+ bounces in 12mo but always cures by Day-15: light IVR only, don't burn agent capacity.
  • Value tiering (high-balance/high-LGD vs long-tail) Top 10% of bucket-90+ holds 62% of exposure → daily senior-collector attention; long-tail → quarterly bulk-notice sweep.

2.2 Strategy engine

  • Treatment-tree configuration — per segment, sequence of actions across days / buckets Soft-bucket tree: T-3 SMS → T+1 IVR → T+5 agent call → T+10 field visit → T+15 demand notice. Configurable by ops, no release needed.
  • Conditional branching — if PTP made → suspend escalation; if RNR×3 → switch channel Day-7: PTP captured for 21-Apr → escalation paused until 22-Apr. PTP broken → jumps to legal-prep node, skips remaining soft steps.
  • Cost-aware action selection — cheapest channel that achieves expected lift IVR ₹0.30/call vs agent ₹35/call. For low-value soft-bucket cases, two IVR attempts before any agent dial.
  • Cool-off and frequency caps embedded in the tree, not in policy docs Max 3 outbound voice attempts/day; max 1 SMS/day. Dialer refuses 4th call automatically — RBI cap enforced at infra, not honor system.

2.3 Allocation engine

  • Auto-assignment to channel (digital / tele / field / legal / agency) Bucket 1-30 + ticket <₹10L → digital; 31-60 → tele; 61-90 → field; 90+ → legal+field. Re-runs nightly on bucket migration.
  • Agent-level allocation respecting skill, language, geography, workload Hindi-speaking agent for Bhiwadi cases; female agent flag for Mrs. Verma's case (request on file); cap 80 cases/agent/day.
  • Agency allocation with quota, vintage-mix, exclusion rules Agency "XYZ Recoveries" gets only 60-90 bucket, max 200 cases/month, never any case with prior harassment complaint.
  • Re-allocation triggers — no-contact in N days, PTP broken, escalation No contact in 5 days → reassign from tele to field; PTP broken twice → escalate from field to legal team.

2.4 Prioritisation

  • Within an agent's queue, ranked by expected-value (P-recovery × amount × time-decay) A ₹4L case at 80% pay-prob (EV ₹3.2L) outranks a ₹10L case at 25% pay-prob (EV ₹2.5L). Agent's top-of-queue is always the highest-EV next call.
  • Next-Best-Action surface — tell the agent what to do, don't make them think On opening LN10234 the desktop says: "Send payment link via WhatsApp — borrower paid via WA last 3 times." One-click action.

2.5 Champion-challenger

  • Run two strategies on matched cohorts simultaneously and measure lift 50% of 31-60 bucket gets new WhatsApp voice-note treatment, 50% gets old IVR. After 30 days, cure-rate +4.2pp at p=0.03 → auto-promote.
Why first-principles: action without strategy is spam. Strategy without measurement is superstition. The decide layer is what separates a collection system from a dialer.
JOB 03

Reach — Establish Contact

Treatment fails if the borrower is unreachable. Reach is its own engineering problem.

3.1 Contact discovery & enrichment

  • Skip-tracing connectors — bureau, telco, social, alternate-data vendors Mobile-1 dead 30 days → CIBIL pull surfaces alternate mobile; EPFO pull confirms current employer; Aadhaar e-KYC consent flow returns latest address.
  • Reference-walking workflow — call references captured at origination, with consent Borrower untraceable → call reference-1 (uncle): "We're trying to reach Mr. Sharma about a routine matter, can you ask him to call back?" — fully scripted, recorded, FPC-compliant.
  • Address verification via field-visit feedback loop FOS visit reports "address vacant, neighbours say family moved 3mo ago" → flag triggers skip-trace order + bureau-address refresh.
  • Co-applicant / guarantor cascade when primary unreachable Mr. Sharma RNR 5 days → system automatically queues Mrs. Sharma (co-applicant) → if she also fails, brother (guarantor) is queued next.

3.2 Channel orchestration

  • All outbound channels first-class — SMS, email, WhatsApp (BSP-integrated, template-managed), IVR, voice-bot, agent, field, letter, legal notice SMS via DLT-registered template EMIRMD-001; WhatsApp via Karix BSP template collection_emi_due_v3; both stored in the template registry, not hard-coded.
  • Channel waterfall — try cheapest, fall back WhatsApp (₹0.10) → if not delivered in 4hrs, IVR (₹0.30) → if RNR, agent (₹35). Each step is a config row, not a code branch.
  • Per-channel suppression — DND, complaint-history, regulatory Borrower on DND → skip voice; in 7-day complaint cool-off → skip all outbound; on legal-hold → only legal team can contact.

3.3 Right-time engine

  • Best-time-to-call per borrower, learned from connect history Mr. Sharma: 87% historical pickup Tue-Thu 11am-1pm → dialer auto-schedules within that window, ignores agent's "call now" instinct.
  • Calling-hour guardrails enforced by jurisdiction (RBI FPC: 8am–7pm) Outbound dialer disabled 7pm–8am IST and all-day on national holidays — enforced at the dialer infra layer; no agent can manually override.

3.4 Identity & deliverability

  • Sender ID / DLT template / WhatsApp template registry All SMS sender IDs and templates registered on TRAI DLT; rejected templates surface in a queue for re-approval; no rogue messages can leave the system.
  • Bounce / undeliverable feedback loop closing back into 1.4 (Contactability state) Email to borrower bounced "user unknown" → state 1.4 marks email invalid → strategy engine drops email channel for this borrower until refresh.
JOB 04

Act — Execute the Treatment

Each channel needs a real execution surface, not just a pointer.

4.1 Digital self-service (pre-due + soft bucket)

  • Dynamic payment links — UPI, cards, netbanking, wallet — deep-linked to amount WhatsApp message: "Pay ₹19,180 EMI for LN10234" with one-tap link → opens GPay/PhonePe pre-filled — no typing, no copy-paste, no errors.
  • WhatsApp pay-flow with inline payment Borrower replies "PAY" → bot sends interactive button → tap → UPI intent → done in ~30 seconds, no app switch.
  • E-NACH / e-mandate — setup, modify, cancel Borrower's HDFC mandate bouncing 3 months in a row → app prompts "Update mandate" → e-mandate via Aadhaar OTP, switches to ICICI salary account.
  • PDC inventory + presentation calendar + bounce-charge automation 32 PDCs in custody; 3 due 5-May; on bounce, ₹500 + GST charge auto-debited and SMS sent the same day.
  • Self-service portal / app — dues, statement, hardship, restructure, callback, dispute Borrower opens app → sees ₹19,180 due → taps "Apply for hardship" → uploads job-loss letter → case routed to restructure queue, no agent dial needed.

4.2 Tele-calling

  • Agent desktop — 360 view + script + disposition + NBA + payment-link send + recording control One screen: borrower 360, last 5 interactions, recommended script, disposition codes, "Send payment link" button, mute/hold/transfer. No tab-hopping.
  • Predictive / progressive / preview dialer modes Predictive for soft bucket high volume; progressive for hard bucket sensitive cases; preview mode for legal/escalation where agent must read the file first.
  • IVR & voice-bot with payment-intent capture and warm-transfer to live agent AI bot: "Mr. Sharma, your ₹19,180 EMI is due. Press 1 to pay now via UPI, 2 to talk to an agent." If 2, transferred to agent with intent already on screen.
  • Call-back scheduler honoured by the dialer Borrower says "call after 3pm" → dialer schedules at 3:05pm and refuses to dial earlier; honours the commitment, builds trust.
  • Live monitoring — whisper, barge for supervisors Supervisor whispers ("offer the OTS option") to a new agent mid-call without borrower hearing; barges in if script goes off-policy.

4.3 Field operations (FOS)

  • Mobile app (offline-capable) with day's beat plan Agent in low-signal village near Khairthal — beat plan, borrower 360, receipts all cached locally; sync when network returns. No "no-network, no-work".
  • Geo-tagged check-in / check-out, selfie + property photo Visit logged at lat 28.21 / lng 76.85 (matches property record ±50m); selfie + property photo timestamped — proof against fake-visit fraud.
  • Route optimisation across the day's cases 8 cases scheduled today across Bhiwadi → Tijara → Khairthal — app sequences for shortest path; ~28km saved, 2 extra cases possible.
  • On-spot receipt — cash, cheque, UPI, POS — digital + printed Borrower pays ₹19,180 cash on doorstep → digital receipt sent to WhatsApp + printed copy from agent's Bluetooth printer; both bear receipt number, GST.
  • Cash custody — deposit slip, deposit-to-bank reconciliation, shortage tracking EOD: Agent Ravi declares ₹47,500 collected; bank deposit slip ₹47,500; matched. Agent Suresh declared ₹32,000; deposit ₹31,500 → ₹500 shortage flagged for HR.
  • Visit disposition with structured outcomes Dropdown: Met-borrower, Met-family, Premises-locked, Address-incorrect, Refused, PTP-given, Paid. Drives strategy tree, not free-text guesswork.
  • Possession workflow (SARFAESI 13(4)) — symbolic and physical 60-day 13(2) reply window elapsed → 13(4) symbolic possession: newspaper notice (English + vernacular), panchnama with two witnesses, photo-record of premises.

4.4 Legal & recovery

  • Notice templates — reminder, demand, Sec 138 NI, SARFAESI 13(2)/13(4)/13(8), possession One-click "Generate SARFAESI 13(2)" → pulls borrower, property, dues; outputs PDF in regulator-compliant format with vernacular translation; lawyer just reviews.
  • E-stamp + e-sign integration; physical dispatch with AD/POD tracking ₹100 e-stamp via SHCIL API; lawyer e-signs via DSC; Speed Post dispatch with AD; AD card scanned and linked back to the case file.
  • Court case management — Sec 138 NI, SARFAESI 17 (DRT appeal), civil suit, arbitration, Lok Adalat, DRT, NCLT Case CC/123/2026 Bhiwadi MM Court, Sec 138 NI; next hearing 12-May; auto-reminders to lawyer + collections lead 7d/3d/1d before; orders auto-attached.
  • Auction module — valuation, reserve price, e-auction, EMD, bidder management, sale certificate, proceeds waterfall ₹24L property reserve set; e-auction on MSTC 15-Mar; 3 bidders, ₹2.4L EMD each; sold ₹26.5L → ₹16.8L to loan, ₹4.2L charges, ₹5.5L surplus to borrower.
  • Lawyer / agency assignment with cost tracking per case Each case has a running cost meter — court fees ₹3,200 + lawyer fee ₹15,000 + misc ₹1,800 = ₹20,000 spent so far; visible alongside outstanding.

4.5 Settlement & restructuring

  • OTS / waiver workflow with delegation matrix — who can approve what haircut Borrower offers ₹12L on ₹16L outstanding (25% haircut). Matrix: ≤25% → Zonal Head; 25–40% → Risk Committee; >40% → Board. Auto-routes, no email chains.
  • Restructure — revised schedule, NPV impact, regulatory classification check EMI ₹19,180 → ₹14,500 for 24mo, balance tenor extended; NPV impact ₹2.4L; system flags as "Restructured Standard" per RBI norms with effective date.
  • Re-aging eligibility check + audit reason code Post-restructure 12 EMIs paid on time → eligible for re-classification to Standard; system alerts and creates approval task with mandatory reason code.
  • Insurance-claim trigger — death, disability, property — claim filing + proceeds appropriation Borrower deceased → death certificate uploaded → claim auto-filed with HDFC Life accident-cover policy → ₹18L claim → applied to loan, surplus to nominee.
JOB 05

Record — Capture What Happened

Without an evidentiary record, no recovery action survives challenge.

5.1 Interaction log

  • Every call (with recording), SMS, email, WhatsApp, visit, letter, notice — timestamped, agent-stamped, channel-stamped 15-Apr 14:32 IST — Outbound call by Agent Ravi (ID 8821) to mobile-1, duration 4:12, recording S3 path stored, disposition PTP-21-Apr-₹19180.
  • Free-text + structured disposition + standardised reason codes Note: "Borrower says salary delayed by employer until 25-Apr"; tagged Hardship + Income-disruption. Free-text supports nuance; codes drive strategy.
  • Customer voice — complaints, hardship statements — tagged separately and never auto-deleted Inbound complaint 18-Apr: "Agent called my neighbour and disclosed loan details" → flagged as harassment complaint, queued to grievance officer with 7-day SLA.

5.2 Promise-to-Pay

  • PTP capture — amount + date + channel Captured during call: PTP ₹19,180 by 21-Apr via UPI. Promise stored as a structured object, not buried in free-text notes.
  • PTP tracking — kept, broken, partial 21-Apr 23:59 — no payment landed → PTP marked broken at midnight → strategy engine auto-escalates to next stage on 22-Apr morning.
  • Auto-link of incoming payment to open PTP Inbound UPI ₹19,180 from same VPA on 22-Apr → auto-links to broken PTP, marks paid-late, agent doesn't have to manually reconcile.

5.3 Document vault

  • All notices, AD cards, POD, court orders, valuation reports, sale certificates, OTS letters, NOCs Sec 138 notice scanned + AD card scanned; SARFAESI 13(2) PDF + Speed Post POD; valuation report (Apr-2026); court order date-stamped, OCR-indexed.
  • Versioning, retention policy, legal-hold flag OTS letter v1 issued, borrower negotiated, v2 issued — both retained, v2 marked active. Court case filed → all account documents flagged legal-hold, cannot be auto-purged.

5.4 Audit trail

  • Append-only log of who did what when — config change, allocation change, waiver, write-off, sensitive PII access 22-Apr 11:14 — User MGR-401 changed waterfall config from "penal-first" to "principal-first" on product AHFL-HF-Std. Approved by RISK-001 at 11:18.
  • Tamper-evident — hash-chained or WORM Each event hash-chained with the previous; tamper attempt detected at next reconciliation. Stands up in DRT/court as digital evidence under IT Act §65B.
JOB 06

Settle — Move and Reconcile Money

The act of collecting is incomplete until money is in the right ledger account.

6.1 Receipt

  • Multi-mode receipt issue — cash, cheque, DD, UPI, NEFT/IMPS/RTGS, card, wallet, payment link, NACH, auction proceeds, insurance claim Same loan, multiple receipt types in one month: ₹19,180 cash at branch counter; ₹19,180 UPI via WhatsApp link; ₹26.5L auction proceeds; ₹18L insurance claim — all flow through the same receipt system.
  • Provisional receipt (cheque-in-clearing) → final receipt on realisation Cheque #128341 ₹19,180 deposited 14-Apr → provisional receipt issued; cleared 16-Apr → final receipt; if bounced 16-Apr → reverse + ₹500 bounce charge raised same day.

6.2 Payment posting

  • Appropriation waterfall (charges → penal → interest → principal), configurable per product ₹19,180 received → ₹680 penal first → ₹4,200 interest → ₹14,300 principal (per product config). Different products can have different waterfalls; never hard-coded.
  • Excess / suspense handling Borrower paid ₹50,000 against ₹19,180 due → ₹19,180 to current EMI, ₹30,820 parked in suspense awaiting borrower instruction (prepay vs hold).
  • Reversal on cheque bounce / chargeback with downstream un-do Cheque bounce → reverse ₹19,180 from EMI → restore DPD to 21 → re-open the case → reverse PTP-kept flag → re-trigger strategy from the right node. No manual cleanup.

6.3 Reconciliation

  • Bank-statement-to-receipt match — auto + exception queue Daily HDFC virtual-account file → auto-match 1,247 receipts; 23 exceptions (amount mismatch, unknown VA) queued for ops review with side-by-side compare.
  • Cash custody reconciliation per FOS / branch / agency Agent Ravi declared ₹47,500; deposit slip ₹47,500 — matched. Agent Suresh declared ₹32,000; deposit ₹31,500 → ₹500 shortage flagged, escalated to HR.
  • PG / NACH file reconciliation NPCI NACH file: 4,210 presented, 3,892 success, 318 bounce → bounce file auto-loaded, ₹500 bounce charge raised per case, strategy engine notified to switch channel.
  • GL posting with sub-ledger tie-out Each receipt posts to GL — interest-income, principal-reduction, penal-income — and sub-ledger balances tie out to GL daily; auditor's first ask, ready out-of-the-box.

6.4 Settlement accounting

  • OTS waiver booking to P&L with approval reference ₹4L waived on OTS → P&L charge with reference OTS-2026-0892, approved by Zonal Head Mr. Mehta. Approval chain attached to the journal entry.
  • Write-off entry with reason code; recovery from written-off booked to other-income Account written off ₹2.1L Mar-2026, reason UNTRACEABLE+TITLE-DISPUTE; in Apr-2026 ₹50,000 recovered from auction proceeds → booked to "other income — w/o recovery".
  • Provisioning automation per RBI/NHB IRAC norms Bucket migration triggers provisioning: 31-60 → 0%, 61-90 → 0.4%, 90+ NPA → 15% sub-standard, >12mo → 25% doubtful-1, >24mo → 40% doubtful-2.
JOB 07

Improve — Measure and Learn

A collection system that doesn't get smarter month-on-month is decaying.

7.1 Operational metrics

  • Roll, flow, bounce, resolve, cure, recovery — sliced by bucket × segment × strategy × geography × agent × agency Bucket 1-30 cure-rate dropped from 78% to 71% in last 30 days for self-employed segment in Tier-3 cities → drill-down shows it's concentrated in Bhiwadi cluster → investigate.
  • Right-party-contact rate, PTP-kept rate, PTP-to-payment lag Agent Ravi: RPC 64% vs team avg 52%; PTP-kept 41% vs team avg 38%; avg PTP→payment lag 2.1 days. Surfaces both stars and stragglers automatically.
  • Cost-to-collect per ₹ recovered, by channel WhatsApp ₹2 per ₹100 recovered; tele ₹18; field ₹85. Strategy engine must reflect this — route low-value cases to cheap channels first, every time.

7.2 People metrics

  • Agent productivity — calls, AHT, cure ₹, QA score, schedule adherence Agent Ravi today: 142 calls, AHT 3:48, cure ₹4.2L, QA 87/100, schedule adherence 96%. Drives daily huddle, monthly appraisal, training assignments.
  • Agency scorecard and payout calc Agency XYZ: allocation ₹2.4Cr, recovery ₹38L, recovery-rate 15.8% vs benchmark 18% → payout slab kicks in: 5% on ₹0–10L recovered, 7% above.

7.3 Strategy lab

  • Champion-challenger results, statistical significance, auto-promote winning variant Variant B (WhatsApp voice-note) lifted cure-rate by 4.2pp at p=0.03 vs Variant A (IVR) → auto-promoted to champion; Variant A archived for rollback.
  • What-if simulator on historical data before rolling a new strategy Run new strategy against last 6mo data → projected ₹12L incremental recovery, ₹1.8L extra channel cost → +ROI 5.7×; confidence interval shown so it isn't sold as a point estimate.

7.4 Regulatory & MIS

  • NHB / RBI return automation — NPA, restructure, write-off, customer-complaints Quarterly NPA return auto-generated from the event stream — no manual extraction; auditor sees raw events behind every line item; cuts compliance prep from 2 weeks to 2 days.
  • Board / risk-committee dashboards GNPA, NNPA, recovery-rate, vintage curves, top-10 large exposures — refreshed nightly; CEO opens at 7am with coffee, doesn't wait for finance to email a deck.
  • Vintage curves, static-pool analysis FY24 originations show 90+ DPD at 2.1% by month-12 vs FY23 at 1.4% → tighten underwriting on similar borrower profile (self-employed, Tier-3, ticket >₹15L).
CROSS-CUTTING

Guardrails — Compliance, Consent, Security

Not features added on top — constraints baked into every other feature.

Regulatory compliance

  • RBI Fair Practices Code: calling hours, language, decency, no third-party harassment
  • RBI Digital Lending Directions (2025): KFS, cooling-off, consent, data localisation, no dark patterns
  • NHB Directions for HFCs: NPA recognition, recovery agent code, complaint handling
  • IBA / IIBF certified-agent registry; certification expiry tracking
  • Recording-consent capture before recording
  • Per-borrower frequency cap (max-N-calls/day) enforced at the dialer, not in policy

Privacy & security

  • Field-level PII masking in agent UI based on role
  • Tokenised storage of bank account / card / PAN
  • Consent ledger (DEPA-aligned) with revocation propagation
  • Access control: role + attribute-based, time-bound, geofenced for FOS app
  • Data retention & purge per data-class

Customer redressal

  • Complaint capture, SLA timers, ombudsman escalation path
  • Hardship channel that bypasses normal collection treatment
  • Internal grievance redressal officer routing
CROSS-CUTTING

Plumbing — Integrations, Identity, Configuration

Without these the module is an island.

Integrations (must-have)

  • LOS / LMS — bidirectional account state
  • Core banking / GL
  • Credit bureaus — CIBIL, CRIF, Equifax, Experian (pull and report)
  • Payment rails: NACH (NPCI), UPI/collect, PG, BBPS, cards
  • Telephony: SIP, dialer, recording storage
  • Messaging: SMS aggregator + DLT, WhatsApp BSP, email ESP
  • Account aggregator (consent-based bank statements)
  • Bureau alerts / alternate-data signals (income, employment, recharge proxies)
  • E-sign / e-stamp / digital-locker
  • Court / e-auction platforms
  • HRMS for agent master + skill + roster

Master data & identity

  • Single canonical borrower ID across products
  • Product, charge, fee, penal-rate, template masters with effective dating
  • Reason-code taxonomy — single source for dispositions, write-off, complaints

Configuration as a product capability

  • Business-user config: strategy trees, allocation rules, dunning calendars, templates, scorecard weights, waterfall, delegation, SLA — without code change
  • Versioning, approval workflow, sandbox-test-then-promote on every config change
  • Config diff and rollback

Platform qualities

  • Multi-entity / multi-product / multi-currency from day one
  • API-first — every UI action available as API
  • Event-driven extensibility — webhook on bucket roll, on PTP, on receipt
  • Observability: per-feature SLOs, event-replay capability
// AHFC-Specific

What Genuinely Changes for Affordable Housing Finance

The seven jobs apply to any lender. AHFC has four extras that change requirements (not just thresholds). They raise the bar on jobs 1, 3, 4, and 5.

01
Mortgage as Collateral

SARFAESI workflow + property valuation + auction module are core, not optional. Property auction is the AHFC's repossession.

02
Long Tenor (15–30 yrs)

Contactability decays over the loan life. Periodic KYC / contact-refresh is a feature, not a project.

03
Informal-Income Borrowers

Field collections dominate. FOS module quality is the single biggest lever on cure rate.

04
Household Liability

The "borrower" is a household — applicant + co-applicant + guarantor. Data model must reflect that everywhere.

// Anti-Features

What NOT to Build

First-principles thinking is also subtraction. Common bloat to avoid:

  • A second source of truth for the loan ledgerThe LMS owns it; collections projects from it.
  • A bespoke dialer when a best-of-breed existsIntegrate, don't rebuild.
  • A "CRM" layer duplicating customer 360One borrower record, many views.
  • Static reports the strategy team can't modifyReports from the event stream = live.
  • Hard-coded strategy treesEvery rule must be config; otherwise every change is a release.
  • Vanity MLA model that doesn't change an action isn't a feature.
  • Over-modelled "case" abstractionCases are a projection over account + borrower + events, not a primitive.
// Application

How to Use This for Vendor Evaluation

For Omnifin, Spocto, AbleCredit, Kriyam, and adjacent players:

  1. Jobs 1–7 are must-cover axes. Mark Y / Partial / N per feature.
  2. Guardrails & Plumbing are disqualifiers. A gap here is rarely fixable post-purchase.
  3. AHFC-Specific section is the fit lens. A vendor strong on unsecured PL but weak on SARFAESI / auction is the wrong shortlist.
  4. The MVP cut is the negotiation anchor. Don't pay enterprise prices for anything beyond MVP unless it passes a measurable-lift test.
  5. Anti-features section is scope discipline. Push back on vendor "value-adds" that don't map to a job.
// Glossary

Terms Used in This Document

Acronyms and domain terms that appear in the feature list, grouped by area. Definitions are scoped to the way the term is used in Indian retail / housing-finance collections.

Risk & Credit

DPD Days Past Due
Number of days an EMI is overdue. Drives bucket assignment.
EMI Equated Monthly Instalment
Fixed monthly repayment of principal + interest over the loan tenor.
Bucket
DPD-based account categorisation: 0, 1-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+, NPA, written-off.
NPA Non-Performing Asset
Account where principal/interest is overdue >90 days; triggers higher provisioning.
GNPA / NNPA
Gross / Net NPA — total bad-loan stock, before and after provisioning.
PD Probability of Default
Likelihood a borrower defaults within a horizon (typically 12 months).
EAD Exposure at Default
Outstanding amount at the moment a default is recognised.
LGD Loss Given Default
Share of EAD the lender expects to lose after recovery. Low for AHFC because loans are mortgage-secured.
EL Expected Loss
EL = PD × LGD × EAD. Drives provisioning and pricing.
ECL Expected Credit Loss
Forward-looking loss estimate under Ind AS 109.
IRAC
RBI's Income Recognition, Asset Classification & Provisioning norms — defines NPA and provisioning slabs.
NPV Net Present Value
Discounted value of future cash flows; used to measure restructuring impact.
ROI Rate of Interest
Annual interest rate applied to the loan principal.

Collections Operations

PTP Promise to Pay
Borrower's commitment to pay a specific amount by a specific date.
P-recovery Probability of Recovery
Model-estimated probability that a delinquent case will be recovered within a horizon. Used for queue prioritisation.
Propensity-to-Pay / Cure / Roll
Model scores for: paying this EMI, returning to current bucket, or rolling forward to a worse bucket.
RPC Right Party Contact
Successful contact with the borrower (not a family member). RPC% is a primary collections KPI.
RNR Ring No Response
Outbound call rang but was not answered.
AHT Average Handle Time
Average duration an agent spends on a call including hold and wrap.
NBA Next Best Action
The single recommended action surfaced to the agent or system at this moment.
Roll Rate
% of accounts moving from one bucket to the next worse bucket in a period.
Flow Rate
% of new entrants into a bucket relative to the prior bucket size — leading indicator.
Cure Rate
% of delinquent accounts that return to current (0 DPD) within a window.
OTS One-Time Settlement
Negotiated settlement where borrower pays a discounted lump sum to close the account.
Re-aging
Re-classifying a previously delinquent account back to standard after sustained on-time payment.
Champion-Challenger
A/B test framework that runs current ("champion") strategy against a variant ("challenger") on matched cohorts.
Skip-tracing
Process of locating a borrower whose contact details have gone stale.
FOS Field Officer / Feet on Street
Field collections agent who visits borrowers in person.
FPC Fair Practices Code
RBI's mandatory code on lender conduct — calling hours, decency, no harassment.

Payments & Banking

UPI Unified Payments Interface
NPCI's instant retail payment rail; primary digital collection channel in India.
VPA Virtual Payment Address
UPI handle (e.g., name@bank) used as the destination for payments.
NACH National Automated Clearing House
NPCI rail for bulk recurring debits (EMIs); replaces ECS.
e-NACH / e-mandate
Electronic mandate set up via Aadhaar OTP or net-banking; enables auto-debit of EMIs.
NPCI
National Payments Corporation of India — operator of UPI, NACH, IMPS.
NEFT / IMPS / RTGS
RBI / NPCI inter-bank transfer rails — batched, instant, and high-value real-time respectively.
BBPS Bharat Bill Payment System
NPCI rail for bill payments including loan EMIs.
PG Payment Gateway
Third-party service that brokers cards / netbanking / wallet payments to a merchant.
PDC Post-Dated Cheque
Cheque dated for future presentation; legacy EMI mechanism, fading.
GL General Ledger
The lender's master accounting ledger; all collections must reconcile back to it.
POS Point of Sale
Card-payment terminal; FOS agents may carry mobile POS for on-spot collection.
Waterfall
Configured order in which a payment is appropriated: typically charges → penal → interest → principal.

Channels & Communication

IVR Interactive Voice Response
Automated voice menu that interacts with the borrower without an agent.
Voice-bot
AI-driven conversational voice interface; can capture intent and warm-transfer to an agent.
BSP Business Solution Provider
Authorised partner that brokers WhatsApp Business API access (e.g., Karix, Gupshup).
DLT Distributed Ledger Telecom
TRAI's registry of approved SMS sender IDs and templates; non-registered SMS gets blocked.
DND Do Not Disturb
TRAI registry of phone numbers opted out of commercial calls / SMS.
Predictive / Progressive / Preview Dialer
Three dialer modes — auto-dial multiple lines, dial-on-agent-availability, agent-reviews-then-dials.
Whisper / Barge
Supervisor coaching modes: whisper (only agent hears) and barge (joins live call).
SLA Service Level Agreement
Time-bound commitment to act on a case (e.g., complaint response within 7 days).

Legal & Recovery

SARFAESI
Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002 — lets secured creditors enforce security without court.
Section 13(2)
SARFAESI demand notice to defaulting borrower; 60-day reply window before further action.
Section 13(4)
SARFAESI possession (symbolic or physical) of secured asset after 13(2) period lapses.
Section 13(8)
SARFAESI sale notice — 30-day notice before auction.
Section 14
SARFAESI provision to seek District Magistrate assistance in taking physical possession.
Section 17
SARFAESI appeal mechanism to DRT against bank's enforcement action.
Section 138 NI Act
Negotiable Instruments Act provision making cheque dishonour a criminal offence.
DRT Debt Recovery Tribunal
Specialised tribunal for bank/FI debt recovery suits and SARFAESI appeals.
NCLT National Company Law Tribunal
Forum for insolvency resolution under IBC; relevant for corporate borrowers and personal guarantors.
Lok Adalat
Statutory people's court for amicable settlement; binding award, no appeal.
AD / POD
Acknowledgement Due / Proof of Delivery — postal evidence that a notice was delivered.
e-stamp
Electronic stamp paper purchased via SHCIL; replaces physical stamp for legal documents.
e-sign / DSC
Electronic signature via Aadhaar OTP / Digital Signature Certificate; legally valid under IT Act.
Panchnama
Witnessed record of a possession or seizure event; required under SARFAESI 13(4).
Reserve Price
Minimum acceptable bid in an auction, set against the property valuation.
EMD Earnest Money Deposit
Refundable deposit a bidder must pay to participate in an auction.
IT Act §65B
Indian Evidence Act provision governing admissibility of electronic records as evidence.

Regulatory Bodies & Frameworks

RBI
Reserve Bank of India — primary regulator for banks and NBFCs / HFCs.
NHB National Housing Bank
Apex housing-finance regulator (now under RBI); issues HFC-specific directions.
HFC / AHFC
Housing Finance Company / Affordable Housing Finance Company — the latter focuses on low-ticket borrowers in Tier-2/3 markets.
KFS Key Fact Statement
RBI-mandated standard disclosure of loan terms before sanction.
Digital Lending Directions 2025
RBI's framework on digital lending — KFS, cooling-off, consent, no dark patterns.
IIBF / IBA
Indian Institute of Banking and Finance / Indian Banks' Association — recovery agents must hold IIBF certification.
TRAI
Telecom Regulatory Authority of India — operates DLT and DND regimes.
DEPA
Data Empowerment and Protection Architecture — India's consent framework underlying Account Aggregator.
Account Aggregator
RBI-licensed framework for consent-based sharing of financial data (e.g., bank statements).
IBC
Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016 — adjudicates corporate and individual insolvency.
Ind AS 109
Indian Accounting Standard governing financial-instrument classification and ECL provisioning.

Systems & Identifiers

LMS Loan Management System
System of record for the loan book — schedule, ledger, accruals.
LOS Loan Origination System
System that handles application, underwriting, and disbursal.
CRM Customer Relationship Management
System for managing customer interactions; collections often pulls from but doesn't duplicate it.
MIS Management Information System
Reporting and dashboard layer for management and regulators.
KYC / e-KYC
Know Your Customer — identity verification process, electronic via Aadhaar.
PAN
Permanent Account Number — issued by Income Tax Dept; primary financial ID in India.
Aadhaar
UIDAI 12-digit unique ID; basis for biometric / OTP-based verification.
CIBIL / CRIF / Equifax / Experian
The four RBI-licensed credit information companies (bureaus).
NSDL
National Securities Depository Limited — also operates PAN verification.
SHCIL
Stock Holding Corporation of India — central record-keeper for e-stamping.
MSTC
Govt-owned e-auction platform widely used for SARFAESI auctions.
EPFO
Employees' Provident Fund Organisation — used for employer / income verification.
WORM Write Once Read Many
Storage that disallows modification after write; used for tamper-evident audit trails.
NOC No Objection Certificate
Lender's release confirming no further claim on a property after loan closure.

Statistical & Analytics

p-value
Probability that an observed result is due to chance under the null hypothesis; lower = more confident.
pp percentage points
Absolute difference between two percentages — e.g., 78% to 71% is a 7pp drop, not "7% drop".
Vintage Analysis
Tracking delinquency by origination cohort (e.g., FY24 originations) to compare quality across periods.
Static Pool
A fixed cohort of accounts tracked over time without additions — clean view of true performance.
EV Expected Value
P-recovery × Amount × Time-decay; used to rank cases in agent queues.
